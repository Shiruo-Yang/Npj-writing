## How to apply this file

- Treat these rules as hard submission constraints.
- Use corpus-derived NPJ style only after these checks pass.
- If the user is writing an `Article`, use the Article rules by default.
- If the user is writing a `Review`, `Perspective`, `Brief Communication`, `Comment`, `Editorial`, or `Matters Arising`, switch to the corresponding content-type matrix below.
- Do not infer 2PI, photochemistry, ML, QM, or materials facts from these official pages. They only govern journal fit, content structure, reporting, and formatting.

## Journal scope and fit

`npj Computational Materials` considers applied computational approaches for the design of new materials and computational work that improves understanding of existing materials.

Scope-relevant topics include:

- theoretical and simulation approaches for organic and inorganic materials
- emerging materials and sustainable materials
- prediction of material structure and properties
- materials fundamentals, behavior, degradation, and properties
- computational findings integrated with experimental validation
- high-throughput computational or experimental data generation
- materials data mining
- materials design with machine learning or artificial intelligence
- methods and theory for materials modelling and informatics

For the current 2PI manuscript, the strongest fit is computational materials discovery plus machine-learning screening, applicability-domain control, and quantum-chemical triage. Avoid framing it as an experimentally validated photoinitiator discovery unless synthesis and photopolymerization evidence are present.

## Submission package

All submissions should include:

- cover letter
- editable English manuscript file formatted for the intended content type
- figures
- optional Supplementary Information
- relevant reporting checklists when applicable

At initial submission, the journal allows flexible formatting. A combined PDF or Word file containing text and figures is encouraged for review. If figures are inserted, keep them legible and place legends near the relevant figure where possible. LaTeX is acceptable at acceptance stage, but compiled PDFs are needed before then.

Revisions must be submitted through the revision link, not as new submissions.

## Cover letter

The cover letter should:

- include corresponding-author affiliation and contact information
- briefly explain context, importance, and journal fit
- avoid repeating the Abstract or Introduction
- disclose confidential information such as conflicts of interest
- disclose related work under consideration or in press
- include suggested or excluded reviewers when useful
- consider reviewer diversity across expertise, geography, gender, ethnicity/race, and career stage

For the current 2PI paper, the cover letter should emphasize mechanism-aware computational prioritization, not an experimental performance claim.

## Article content type

Use `Article` for substantial original primary research. Systematic reviews, scoping reviews, and meta-analyses should also be submitted as Articles, not Reviews.

Article format:

- Title page: title up to 15 words; no punctuation, idioms, or puns; include author list, affiliations with institution/city/country, and corresponding-author email.
- Abstract: unheaded, up to 150 words.
- Introduction: no subheadings.
- Results: subheadings should be used.
- Discussion: no subheadings; no separate Limitations or Conclusions section.
- Methods: subheadings should be used; all method descriptions must be in the main manuscript file, not Supplementary Information.
- Data Availability: mandatory.
- Code availability: required where applicable.
- Acknowledgments: funding goes here; do not create a separate Funding section.
- Author contributions: mandatory; refer to every author by initials.
- Competing interests: mandatory; state if none.
- References: about 60 as a guide, not strictly enforced.
- Figure legends: up to 350 words per figure.
- Supplementary Information: allowed, not edited by the journal, remove tracked changes, combine into one separate PDF where possible.

## Brief Communication

Use for concise, high-quality, broad-interest studies that do not meet the full Article scale.

Format:

- Title page: same 15-word title and author/affiliation requirements as Articles.
- Abstract: unheaded, up to 70 words.
- Main text: no subheadings; about 1,000-1,500 words, excluding abstract, Methods, references and figure legends.
- Methods: subheadings required; all methods in main manuscript file.
- Data Availability: mandatory.
- Code availability: where applicable.
- Acknowledgments: funding goes here.
- Author contributions: mandatory, initials.
- Competing interests: mandatory.
- References: around 20 as a guide.
- Figure legends: up to 350 words per figure.
- Supplementary Information: same PDF and no-edit expectations.

## Comment

Use for immediately relevant issues of policy, science and society, or scientific debate written for broad readership.

Format:

- Title page: same 15-word title and author/affiliation requirements.
- Abstract: unheaded, up to 70 words.
- Main text: subheadings permitted; typically 1,000-2,000 words.
- Acknowledgments: where applicable.
- Author contributions: mandatory, initials.
- Competing interests: mandatory.
- References: typically no more than 25.
- Figure legends: up to 350 words per figure.
- Supplementary Information: allowed, not edited, single PDF where possible.

## Editorial

Editorials are normally written by the senior editorial team; unsolicited Editorials are not considered.

Format:

- Title page: same 15-word title and author/affiliation requirements.
- Abstract: unheaded, up to 70 words.
- Main text: subheadings permitted.
- Acknowledgments: where applicable.
- Author contributions: mandatory, initials.
- Competing interests: mandatory.
- References: up to 60 as a guide, not strictly enforced.
- Figure legends: up to 350 words per figure.

## Matters Arising

Use for comments and clarifications on peer-reviewed articles published in the journal. The journal does not consider Matters Arising on papers from other journals. Replies are linked to the original paper.

Format from content-type page:

- Title page: title up to 15 words; Reply title should use `Reply to: [Matters Arising title]`; author list, affiliations and corresponding-author email required.
- Abstract: unheaded, up to 70 words.
- Main text: subheadings permitted; concise; should not exceed 1,200 words.
- Acknowledgments: where applicable.
- Author contributions: mandatory, initials.
- Competing interests: mandatory.
- References: typically no more than 15; the original paper discussed should be the first reference.
- Figure legends: up to 350 words per figure.

Extra Matters Arising page requirements:

- Start with a brief, non-specialist summary paragraph; use it as the abstract for submission.
- Keep the message simple; one or two small figures or tables is ideal.
- Figures should be good enough for referee assessment, ideally as JPEGs at submission.
- Supplementary Information is at editorial discretion.
- The tone must be measured and not inflammatory.
- Authors are encouraged to contact the original paper's authors before submission and attach any correspondence.

## Perspective

Use for evidence-based opinions, models and ideas that are forward-looking, narrower, or more speculative than a Review, while remaining balanced.

Format:

- Title page: same 15-word title and author/affiliation requirements.
- Abstract: unheaded, up to 70 words.
- Main text: subheadings permitted; normally up to 3,000 words.
- Acknowledgments: where applicable.
- Author contributions: mandatory, initials.
- Competing interests: mandatory.
- References: up to 70 as a guide, not strictly enforced.
- Figure legends: up to 350 words per figure.
- Supplementary Information: allowed, not edited, single PDF where possible.

## Review

Use for narrative summaries of recent advances. Reviews should inform non-specialists, avoid excessive jargon, and not be dominated by one laboratory or the authors' own work. Systematic reviews, scoping reviews and meta-analyses should be Articles instead.

Format:

- Title page: same 15-word title and author/affiliation requirements.
- Abstract: unheaded, up to 70 words.
- Main text: subheadings permitted; typically 3,000-4,000 words.
- Acknowledgments: where applicable.
- Author contributions: mandatory, initials.
- Competing interests: mandatory.
- References: up to 60 as a guide, not strictly enforced.
- Figure legends: up to 350 words per figure.
- Supplementary Information: allowed, not edited, single PDF where possible.

## Collections and special issues

Collections, Guest Edited Collections and Special Issues may contain Articles, Reviews and other accepted content types. Submissions are assessed by normal journal criteria, editorial policies, peer-review standards, and collection scope. Do not loosen manuscript or evidence standards because a paper is intended for a collection.

## Methods requirements

Methods must support interpretation and replication.

Include, as applicable:

- concise Methods subheadings
- standard protocols and procedures
- suppliers for reagents, kits, instrumentation, software, and databases
- synthesis protocols for compounds
- reagent amounts in parentheses when possible
- safety hazards for reagents or protocols
- isolated mass and percent yield at protocol end
- statistics and reproducibility subsection when statistical testing is used
- reagents subsection when relevant
- animal model subsection when relevant
- protocols.io or another protocol repository when useful

For the current computational 2PI manuscript, Methods should cover:

- molecule sources, identifiers, and licenses or access constraints
- SMILES standardization, salt stripping, tautomer/charge handling, deduplication and filtering
- endpoint definitions, missing labels and unit conventions
- train/validation/test split logic, scaffold split, leakage checks and random seeds
- model architecture, hyperparameters, ensemble or replicate design and calibration
- uncertainty, prediction disagreement, applicability domain and OOD handling
- ZINC22 snapshot, query, filtering, role taxonomy and exclusion rules
- fixed pre-QM selection rules and family aggregation
- Gaussian or other QM software version, functional, basis set, solvent model, charge, spin, convergence, failure handling and coordinate provenance

## Reporting summary and editorial policy checklist

If a manuscript is sent for peer review, the journal may require:

- Nature Portfolio Reporting Summary after peer review
- Nature Portfolio Editorial Policy Checklist before formal review
- relevant discipline-specific checklists

Primary research articles in broad Nature Portfolio categories accepted since 2019 may publish Reporting Summary as a supplementary file. Physical sciences research has specific reporting summary requirements in selected areas such as solar cells and claims of lasing.

For the 2PI manuscript, check whether photophysics, laser exposure, polymerization, or materials characterization triggers any discipline-specific reporting expectations even if no human/animal/life-science forms apply.

## References

References must:

- be numbered sequentially in first appearance order across text, Methods, tables and figure legends
- contain one publication per number
- include only published, accepted, recognized preprint, published conference abstract, numbered patent, research dataset or otherwise permitted source types
- cite papers in preparation and unpublished meeting abstracts in text rather than numbered references
- include DOI or accession code for datasets where available
- cite most websites parenthetically in text rather than in the reference list
- avoid grant details or acknowledgements as numbered references
- avoid footnotes
- follow Nature reference style
- list all authors unless there are more than five; then use first author plus `et al.`

## Required statements

Author contributions:

- mandatory for accepted npj Series content
- specify individual contributions for every co-author
- use initials

Acknowledgements:

- brief
- do not thank anonymous referees or editors
- do not use effusive or inessential language
- do not list contributors who meet authorship criteria
- funding information belongs here, not in a separate Funding section
- state the funder's role in design, data collection, analysis, interpretation and manuscript writing
- if no funding was received, state that
- obtain consent from named acknowledged individuals

Competing interests:

- mandatory
- include financial and non-financial interests
- if none, state no competing interests
- declare funder-related conflicts separately from Acknowledgements
- if a journal editor is an author, disclose this and state that they had no role in peer review or decision-making

ORCID:

- corresponding authors must provide ORCID before final accepted manuscript submission
- non-corresponding authors are encouraged to link ORCID
- ORCID cannot be added or modified at proof stage

Authorship:

- authorship carries accountability
- each author should contribute substantially, approve the submitted version, and accept responsibility for their contribution and integrity issues
- corresponding author is responsible for author-list agreement, submission awareness, communication, and competing-interest statements
- writing assistance should be acknowledged when it does not meet authorship criteria
- LLMs do not meet authorship criteria

## Data availability

Data Availability is mandatory for submitted manuscripts reporting original research.

The section must:

- appear after Methods and before References
- identify the minimal dataset needed to interpret, replicate and extend the work
- state repository, persistent link, accession, DOI, restriction reason, or third-party access conditions as applicable
- avoid vague statements when data support claims
- disclose restrictions at submission and in the manuscript
- prefer public repositories for large datasets rather than Supplementary Information

For the 2PI manuscript, plan to cover:

- training data or redistributable derivatives
- source DOI and metadata tables
- molecular identifiers and canonical SMILES
- standardization and filtering rules
- split assignments
- screened ZINC22 subsets or exact query/filter definitions
- candidate portfolios and family assignments
- QM input/output summaries, coordinates and failure logs when shareable
- figure source data

## Code availability

Custom code or scripts central to main claims must be available to editors and referees upon request.

For studies using custom code central to conclusions:

- include a `Code availability` statement in Methods
- state access route and restrictions
- release code at publication when possible
- prefer DOI-minting repositories such as Zenodo or Code Ocean when releasing code
- cite released code appropriately

For the 2PI manuscript, cover:

- preprocessing and standardization scripts
- split construction
- Chemprop/model training and inference
- uncertainty/applicability-domain code
- ZINC22 filtering and mechanism taxonomy
- portfolio selection
- QM parsing and audit scripts
- software versions and environment files

## Materials and protocols availability

Materials, data, code and associated protocols should be available without undue qualification. Restrictions must be disclosed to editors at submission and in the manuscript.

New chemical compounds integral to the conclusions require:

- structure
- synthesis
- characterization
- sufficient experimental detail for reproduction

Unique materials should be made available promptly where possible.

## Supplementary Information

Supplementary Information:

- is sent to referees and published with accepted manuscripts
- is not edited, typeset or proofed by the journal
- should be a separate, single merged PDF where possible
- must remove tracked changes
- must be checked carefully because post-publication changes require formal correction
- must be cited consistently in the manuscript
- should not include `data not shown` placeholders
- must not include Supplementary Methods; all Methods belong in the main manuscript file

Large supplementary tables or Excel files should be submitted as `Supplementary Data`, not `Supplementary Table`, and should have titles and legends in the Supplementary Information PDF.

## Figures and tables

General figure rules:

- avoid unnecessary figures
- use multi-panel figures only when panels are logically connected
- number figures with Arabic numerals in text order
- use error bars where appropriate and define statistical treatment in legends
- submit chemical reactions or experimental sequences as figures, not schemes
- a limited number of uncaptioned chemical-structure graphics is acceptable if each is clearly labeled
- use consistent sans-serif lettering such as Arial or Helvetica
- use white backgrounds
- avoid excessive boxing, decorative effects, low-resolution graphics and misleading 3D-style chart effects
- do not truncate histogram axes to exaggerate differences
- use SI units with a space between number and unit
- use scale bars instead of magnification factors where relevant
- use accessible color palettes and avoid red-green dependence or arbitrary rainbow scales
- make labels readable after reduction

Figure legends:

- include legends in the main manuscript file after References
- keep each legend to 350 words or fewer
- define new abbreviations, symbols, colors and error-bar meanings
- describe each panel individually
- use text descriptions rather than visual symbols alone in legends
- label multi-panel figures with `a), b), c)` convention for npj content-type rules; apply consistently to supplementary figures

Peer-review figure package:

- at initial submission, incorporate figures into the main file when possible
- otherwise upload high-resolution figure files or deposit image data with private reviewer access

Publication figure files:

- upload each complete figure as one separate file
- do not upload panel-by-panel files for a multi-panel figure
- use RGB color and at least 300 dpi for bitmap images
- prefer editable vector or layered files when possible
- accepted formats include common vector, bitmap, PowerPoint and ChemDraw formats

Tables:

- place tables at the end of the text document
- table legends for statistical data should describe error analysis and ranges

Video figures:

- accepted when they support the research
- individual video figure maximum is 150 MB
- total combined content should not exceed 1 GB
- if video is part of a multipart figure, submit the whole multipart figure as video

## Image integrity and AI images

Image integrity:

- retain unprocessed source data and metadata
- list image acquisition tools and processing software in Methods
- document key acquisition settings and processing steps
- do not combine images from different times or locations without clear disclosure
- if juxtaposition is essential, mark borders and explain in the legend
- do not use cloning, healing or other tools that obscure manipulations
- apply brightness/contrast changes equally across the full image and controls
- do not process images so data disappear or one region is emphasized misleadingly

AI policy:

- LLMs and AI tools do not qualify as authors
- LLM use that goes beyond AI-assisted copy editing should be documented in Methods or a suitable section
- AI-assisted copy editing of human-generated text for readability, style, grammar, spelling, punctuation or tone does not need to be declared
- authors remain accountable for the final text
- generative AI images/videos are generally not permitted for publication, except narrow cases reviewed by the journal
- exceptions must be clearly labeled as AI-generated in the image field
- non-generative ML tools used to manipulate, combine or enhance images/figures should be disclosed in the relevant caption for case-by-case review

For the current 2PI project, disclose Codex/LLM involvement if it contributed to writing, analysis, code or decision support beyond copy editing.

## Statistics and reproducibility

If statistical testing is used, Methods must include a statistics and reproducibility section.

Report:

- test name
- n value for each analysis
- comparison of interest
- reason the test is appropriate
- normality or assumption checks where relevant
- alpha level
- one-tailed or two-tailed status
- actual P value, not only threshold labels
- descriptive statistics with n, center and variability
- whether error bars show SD, SEM, confidence intervals, ranges or another quantity
- multiple-comparison handling where relevant
- small-n justification where needed

Do not use `significant` without a P value. Use non-statistical language such as `substantial` only when no formal test supports significance.

For the 2PI manuscript, separate ML evaluation metrics from inferential statistics. Report confidence intervals, replicate seeds, or bootstrap analyses only if actually performed.

## Chemical and materials characterization

The manuscript must provide adequate data to support identity, purity and homogeneity of compounds and materials.

For known compounds central to the study:

- state source
- state identity
- state purity
- state whether purchased, resynthesized or newly synthesized

Chemical nomenclature:

- identify molecular structures by bold Arabic numerals in order of presentation
- after first definition, refer to compounds consistently by IUPAC name, standard abbreviation or bold numeral
- define specialist abbreviations at first use

Synthesis Methods:

- include protocols for synthesized compounds
- provide commercial sources
- identify safety hazards
- report reagent amounts
- report isolated mass and percent yield
- quantify inseparable isomeric ratios when possible
- use protocols.io or another protocol platform when useful

Characterization:

- establish organic/organometallic identity by spectroscopy
- provide purity evidence for each isolated compound
- report melting points for crystalline compounds where relevant
- state when species were not isolated or fully characterized
- provide NMR peak listings for relevant nuclei
- provide UV/IR when relevant
- provide mass spectral evidence; HRMS preferred for small molecules
- for single-crystal XRD, provide CIF and structural information as required
- deposit crystallographic data in appropriate databases when required
- for solid-state materials, use suitable solid-state characterization such as powder XRD or solid-state NMR

For a computational-only 2PI manuscript, do not imply synthesized-compound characterization exists. If purchased candidate follow-up is discussed, identify future source/identity/purity requirements.

## Computational results

For electronic-structure calculations:

- provide atomic coordinates of optimized computational models
- make software/code available and cite it properly
- report input parameters
- report basis sets
- report coordinates of input and output structures
- provide absolute energies in Supplementary Information when relative energies are reported

For molecular dynamics:

- supply at least initial and final configurations
- preferably deposit trajectories or structures in a repository
- otherwise supply supplementary data, ideally plain text

For the 2PI manuscript, this means each QM lane needs enough detail to reproduce the calculation, not only a table of summarized descriptors.

## Plagiarism duplicate publication and related manuscripts

The manuscript must not significantly overlap with other papers from the authors under consideration or in press, except conference abstracts and unrefereed preprints. Related manuscripts with overlapping authorship must be supplied or disclosed. If a related manuscript is submitted elsewhere during review, send it to the editor.

Avoid plagiarism, unacknowledged paraphrase, text recycling and self-plagiarism. Attribute and cite when summarizing prior work. Do not recycle large text blocks from prior manuscripts or from the writing corpus.

## Editorial process and review-facing criteria

npj Series research content is peer reviewed when it meets editorial interest. Manuscripts selected for review usually go to two or three reviewers. Editors consider novelty, conceptual advance, evidence strength, technical soundness, interpretability and journal fit.

A paper may be rejected for specialist interest, lack of novelty, insufficient conceptual advance, major technical problems, major interpretational problems, serious content problems, or policy violations.

For cover letters and internal readiness audits, articulate:

- what the work adds beyond a specialist-screening exercise
- why the evidence chain supports the central claim
- why limitations do not invalidate the contribution
- which data/code/materials are available for review

## Article-ready checklist for the current 2PI manuscript

Before presenting text as NPJ Article-ready, verify:

- Title is 15 words or fewer and free of punctuation, idioms and puns.
- Abstract is one unheaded paragraph of 150 words or fewer.
- Introduction has no subheadings.
- Results has informative subheadings.
- Discussion has no subheadings and no separate Limitations or Conclusions.
- Methods has subheadings and includes all data, ML, screening, taxonomy, selection and QM details in the main manuscript file.
- Data Availability is after Methods and before References.
- Code availability appears when custom code supports central conclusions.
- Acknowledgements contain funding and funding-role statement; no separate Funding section.
- Author contributions and competing interests are present.
- LLM use is disclosed if beyond copy editing.
- References follow Nature numbering and style.
- Supplementary Information is one merged PDF where possible and contains no Supplementary Methods.
- Large tables are Supplementary Data with titles and legends.
- Figures are accessible, high quality and numbered in text order.
- Figure legends are after References and no more than 350 words.
- QM calculations provide software, basis set, input/output coordinates, optimized coordinates and absolute energies where relative energies are reported.
- Claims remain computational-prioritization claims unless experimental photopolymerization evidence exists.
